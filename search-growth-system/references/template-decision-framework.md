# Template Decision Framework (Scale / Improve / Pause)

Use this framework to decide whether a pSEO template family should be expanded now, improved first, or paused.

## 1) Scoring Dimensions

Rate each template family on a 1–10 scale:

1. **Content depth & uniqueness**
2. **Intent match quality**
3. **Metadata quality**
4. **Schema coverage**
5. **Internal linking strength**
6. **Duplication/thin risk** (reverse score: higher = safer)
7. **Observed indexation & impressions**

## 2) Decision Rules

### Scale Now
Use when:
- content is unique and intent-fit is strong,
- duplication risk is low,
- and at least sample URLs are indexed and earning impressions.

### Improve Before Scaling
Use when:
- pattern is strategically valid,
- but quality/schema/internal-links are inconsistent,
- or indexation is weak.

### Pause / De-prioritize
Use when:
- template is structurally thin,
- high duplication risk exists,
- or search demand/strategic fit is weak.

## 3) Suggested Trigger Thresholds

- **Indexation rate < 50%** on a mature sample → Improve first
- **Impressions present but near-zero clicks** → Improve snippets/intent match
- **Repeated “crawled/discovered not indexed”** → Improve content depth/uniqueness
- **Strong indexing + impression spread** → Candidate to scale

## 4) Output Table Format

| Template Family | Depth | Intent | Schema | Duplication Risk | Indexation Signal | Decision | Next Action |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|---|
| Example: Concepts | 9 | 10 | 9 | Low | Medium-High | Scale | Expand to next wave |
| Example: Comparisons | 7 | 9 | 6 | Medium | Low-Medium | Improve | Add unique FAQ + richer body |

## 5) Expansion Guardrail

Do not expand a template family by large multiples unless:
- current batch shows healthy indexation,
- the template has clear unique-value structure,
- and there is bandwidth to refresh low performers.
